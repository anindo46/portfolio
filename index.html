01 — About

Who I Am & What I Do

I am an undergraduate researcher in Geology and Mining at the University of Barishal, with a focus on geospatial analysis, environmental modeling, and climate-related research. My academic work centers on understanding spatial and temporal dynamics of environmental systems using satellite-based observations and GIS techniques.

I have worked on multiple projects involving vegetation analysis, shoreline change detection, and land use transformation. Through these studies, I explore how natural and human-induced processes influence environmental change, using data-driven approaches to extract meaningful patterns from complex geospatial datasets.

My goal is to contribute to research that improves understanding of environmental systems and supports practical solutions for climate adaptation, resource management, and sustainable development — particularly in vulnerable coastal regions like Bangladesh.

GEE · Sentinel-5P · Air Quality
Annual SO₂ Pollution in Dhaka (2024)

Mapped sulfur dioxide (SO₂) pollution across Dhaka using Sentinel-5P TROPOMI satellite imagery processed in Google Earth Engine. The map reveals high-concentration hotspots in industrial and traffic-dense zones with precision color-graded visualization.

  • Identified toxic air hotspots concentrated in central and industrial Dhaka
  • Color-coded from low (blue) to extreme (red) SO₂ levels
  • Supports air quality policy and public health risk assessment
Data: Sentinel-5P TROPOMI  ·  Tools: GEE, ArcGIS  ·  Year: 2024
Landsat-9 · GEE · LST
Land Surface Temperature Change — Jashore (2020–2024)

Using Landsat-9 thermal bands processed in Google Earth Engine, this study measured and visualized the spatial distribution of surface heat during peak summer months (April–May). LST surged from ~24.6°C in 2020 to over 37.8°C in 2024, reflecting intensifying Urban Heat Island effects driven by rapid urbanization and vegetation loss.

  • Detects UHI-prone hotspots across urbanized zones
  • Advocates for green infrastructure to mitigate heat stress
  • Supports climate resilience planning and heat vulnerability mapping
Data: Landsat-9 thermal bands  ·  Tools: GEE, ArcGIS  ·  Period: 2020–2024
GEE · QGIS · NDWI · Time-Series
Wetland Dynamics of Bangladesh (2013–2023)

This project maps the temporal evolution of wetlands, rivers, and floodplains across Bangladesh over a decade using multi-year GEE satellite data. Several areas showed wetland shrinkage from encroachment while others expanded from monsoon flooding and sediment re-channeling.

  • Decade-long NDWI time-series detecting waterbody change
  • Identifies wetland shrinkage, expansion, and hydrological shifts
  • Supports wetland conservation and flood zone resilience planning
Data: Sentinel-2, Landsat via GEE  ·  Tools: GEE, QGIS  ·  Period: 2013–2023
MODIS · GEE · VCI · Drought
VCI Drought Map — Bangladesh (April 2020)

Drought risk mapping using the Vegetation Condition Index (VCI) derived from MODIS MOD13Q1 NDVI (250m) imagery. VCI compares April 2020 vegetation against long-term NDVI averages, identifying extreme-to-moderate drought zones (VCI < 40) in the northwest and central regions.

  • Northwest and central Bangladesh show extreme drought zones
  • Eastern hill tracts and major river corridors remain healthy (VCI > 60)
  • Supports food security monitoring and irrigation planning
Data: MODIS MOD13Q1 NDVI 250m  ·  Tools: GEE, QGIS  ·  Date: April 2020
Sentinel-2 · ArcGIS · LULC
LULC Classification — Bhola & Patuakhali (2024)

Detailed Land Use Land Cover classification for coastal Bhola and Patuakhali districts using Sentinel-2 (10m resolution) imagery from 2024. Supervised training in ArcGIS 10.8 with ground-truth samples — classifying built-up areas, dense/moderate vegetation, fallow land, river bodies, and bare terrain.

  • Tracks coastal expansion, vegetation loss, and urbanization
  • Supports land zoning, disaster risk, and municipal planning
  • Post-classified with smoothing filters and zonal statistics validation
Data: Sentinel-2 10m  ·  Tools: ArcGIS 10.8  ·  Year: 2024
MODIS · Landsat-8/9 · GEE · LST
LST Trend Analysis — Barishal (Jan–Mar 2025)

Land Surface Temperature trend analysis across Barishal for Q1 2025 using GEE with MODIS and Landsat-8/9 thermal imagery. LST ranged from 19.53°C to 32.39°C, with highest concentrations in central built-up zones. Vegetated and water areas remained significantly cooler, highlighting the critical role of urban greenery.

  • LST hotspots concentrated in densely built central Barishal
  • Vegetated and river zones 5–8°C cooler than built-up areas
  • Monthly composites styled in ArcGIS Pro for publication-quality output
Data: MODIS & Landsat-8/9 thermal  ·  Tools: GEE, ArcGIS Pro  ·  Period: Jan–Mar 2025
